# Steadybee — User Journey

The journey is designed to move someone from *anxious stranger* to *renewing customer* by escalating
trust and value one small step at a time. Each step maps to one of the four pillars and to the
emotional state we're managing.

## The arc at a glance

| Step | Stage | What happens | Pillar | Emotion we manage |
|------|-------|--------------|--------|-------------------|
| 0 | Discover | Finds us via a worried Google search or a creator/community | — | Curiosity / worry |
| 1 | Try (no risk) | Plays with the **Bella demo** — no signup, no data | Trust | "Oh, this is useful" |
| 2 | Free check-up | Enters own holdings (CSV or manual) → honest diagnosis | Fear | Productive **doubt** |
| 3 | Bridge + capture | Sees today→future link; leaves email for the full picture | Fear→Trust | "I need to know more" |
| 4 | Pay | Buys the annual **Retirement MOT report** (£99–£150) | Trust | Relief / control |
| 5 | Explore | Uses the **scenario sandbox** (behavioural levers) | Usability | Agency |
| 6 | Stay | Calm, material-change-only nudges between reviews | Reliability | Reassurance |
| 7 | Renew | Re-buys next year's MOT; sees year-on-year + peer move | Ritual | Habit / loyalty |

---

## Step 0 — Discover
- Lands from a **problem-query search** ("is my portfolio too concentrated", "sequence of returns
  risk UK", "am I overpaying fund fees"), a **finance-creator** mention, or a helpful **community**
  post. Not from ads to a cold "can I retire" page.
- First impression must signal **trust and independence**: "we sell nothing; honest second opinion."

## Step 1 — Try before you trust (Bella demo)
- The visitor plays with **"Bella, 60, £400k SIPP"** — sees the check-up and sandbox in action with
  zero personal data surrendered.
- They feel the value *before* the privacy ask. This is the trust on-ramp for scam-wary users.

## Step 2 — The free check-up (where doubt is born)
- They choose **CSV upload or manual entry**, then hit the **confirm-holdings** step ("we read
  these — confirm").
- They get the **money-denominated headline**: *"You look diversified, but 80% rides on one theme;
  in a 2022 repeat you'd lose ~£130k."*
- The doubt is aimed at a **fixable** thing (their allocation/assumptions) — not free-floating dread,
  which would make them close the tab.

## Step 3 — The bridge + email capture
- One sentence connects present to future: *"These weaknesses could push your retirement from 62 to
  65. See your full picture and what would change it."*
- Soft, honest CTA → email capture. Now they're in the **nurture** loop even if they don't buy today.

## Step 4 — Pay (the annual MOT)
- They buy the **Retirement MOT report** for £99–£150/year. Positioned as a *ritual* (like a car MOT
  or tax return), not a subscription to forget about.
- Trust closers at the point of sale: deterministic maths, cited sources, **"AI explains, code
  computes,"** money-back if the report's inputs are wrong, independence, delete-anytime privacy.

## Step 5 — Explore (agency, not anxiety)
- In the report + sandbox they drag **behavioural levers** — save more, work longer, spend less,
  delay state pension — and watch the "money lasts" probability move.
- This converts fear into **agency**: "I can see exactly what I'd need to do." No fund picks, no
  recommendations — illustrative only.

## Step 6 — Stay (calm cadence)
- Between annual reviews, **rare, material-change-only** nudges keep Steadybee gently present:
  *"Your US-tech concentration drifted 15% above your baseline — worth a look."* Facts only.
- The promise is *calm*, so we never weekly-ping anxiety. Presence without pestering.

## Step 7 — Renew (the ritual + the moat)
- A year on, they're invited to re-run their MOT. The new report shows **year-over-year change** and
  their **peer-benchmark movement** ("you're now less concentrated than 62% of similar investors").
- The honest renewal test: *was last year's report worth buying again?* If yes → habit, loyalty,
  and word-of-mouth (the golf-club recommendation that is our best growth channel).

## Worked example — "Margaret, 60, £450k"
1. Googles *"how concentrated is too concentrated"* after a wobble in her tech-heavy ISA → finds a
   Steadybee article (Step 0).
2. Plays with the Bella demo, thinks *"this actually makes sense"* (Step 1).
3. Manually enters her HL + old workplace pension, confirms holdings, sees *"−£140k in a 2022 repeat;
   72% of your equity is US tech"* (Step 2).
4. Reads *"this could push your retirement from 61 to 64"*; leaves her email (Step 3).
5. A week later, nudged by the nurture email, buys the £129 MOT (Step 4).
6. In the sandbox discovers that working 18 more months + trimming concentration herself lifts her
   "money lasts to 95" odds from 79% → 90% (Step 5).
7. Gets one calm nudge in October; ignores the noise, trusts the signal (Step 6).
8. Next June, re-buys to see if she's on track — and tells her sister about it (Step 7).

---

## What success looks like (the metrics that matter, in order)
1. **Pre-sale conversion** (fake-door): do they reach for the wallet before we build?
2. **Upload/entry completion:** do scam-wary users actually give us their data (Bella demo working)?
3. **Return / renewal rate:** the whole business — do they come back and re-buy next year (>60%)?
4. **Referral:** "I'd recommend it" — the golf-club effect.
